Workplace and Eating Habits

Office hours can often make things tough for your diet. Here are some smart ways to keep your health in check, in tandem with your hectic work life!

 

Educate yourself:

There are myths about various foods. By educating yourself, you'll be able to make simple and easy changes to your diet for healthier outcomes and better work performance. 

Ex. eggs on toast make a better breakfast than jam on toast by switching sugar for protein, zinc, iron, vitamin D and the brain-boosting chemical choline.

 

Make a routine:

Make a simple dish that you can take to work. That might also include routinely buy healthy snacks (carrot sticks, nuts and seeds) on your way to work so you don’t end up buying crisps/potato chips and sweets or candy.

And, importantly, make your eating decisions before you get hungry.

 

Don't Skip Meals:

Eating the first meal of the day can encourage your body to burn more calories throughout the day and help contain rising cortisol (the primary ‘stress hormone) levels which are high in the early morning. High levels of cortisol can make you feel anxious or jittery.

 

Smart Lunching:

  • Take time to take your lunch
  • Prepare your lunch at home
  • Find a lunch buddy and/or start a lunch club
  • Keep food at work (dressing, condiment etc.)
  • Don’t eat at your desk - it’s much easier to overeat if you’re distracted
  • Find the healthy eating options near your office
  • Exercise portion control
  • Avoid processed/packaged foods

Stay Hydrated:

It is important to stay hydrated but you should try to do so by drinking water and not sugary or caffeinated drinks as they can have adverse side effects such as dehydration, hyperglycemia and sleep disruption.

 

Healthy Snacking:

Instead of having junk while you feel hungry have these at your desk:

  • Whole grain crackers
  • Rolled oats (oatmeal)
  • Nut butter, such as peanut or almond butter
  • Nuts and seeds, such as almonds or cashews


Poor nutrition doesn’t just impact our health, it can affect our performance at work by negatively impacting concentration and energy levels, and cause irritability, frustration and impatience. So be careful next time you go grocery shopping.
